Introducing Humanity In Action: Grateful Hearts Campaign 2026

It's that time of the year! Launching 8 May 2026, in tandem with World Red Cross Day, Singapore Red Cross presents Grateful Hearts Campaign 2026 — our annual signature fundraiser dedicated to making a difference.

This year's theme, HUMANITY IN ACTION, is a call for everyone to step forward, not as observers of social issues, but as changemakers. In a world where it is easy to overlook challenges, let's choose courage over complacency, and action over apathy.

❤️ Seniors Staying Alone

After her husband passed away, Mdm Ng Ban Wee, 72, was left to manage on her own — and like many seniors living alone, the worry of falling with no one nearby never quite leaves. HoME+, ElderAid, and MCT give seniors like her the safety, companionship, and peace of mind to age with dignity.

  • $80 gives an isolated senior a safe trip to their medical appointment with a trained chaperone.
  • $400 gives a vulnerable senior a full year of regular check-ins, warm companionship, and a friendly defense against the quiet health risk of loneliness.
  • $1,920 gives an elderly individual one year of 24/7 peace of mind, to age safely and independently in their own homes.

❤️ Persons with Disabilities

Vicky is 43, has hydrocephalus, and lives with mobility limitations that make daily life impossible to manage, even with a full-time caretaker. After 20 years of searching, his mother found professional care she could trust at RCHD and the DAC — a place where Vicky belongs, and she can finally breathe. For people like Vicky, round-the-clock care, therapy, and companionship can make all the difference.

  • $500 covers one month of consumables and essentials for one resident — milk feeds, diapers, nursing supplies and medical fees.
  • $2,000 covers one year of care services - nursing, therapy, outings, after-hours emergency ambulance and assistive support.
  • $8,000 covers a resident or individual's care services and essential support for an entire year.

❤️ Families in Need

Some families hit a hard season where putting food on the table becomes the heaviest thing to carry. Meals with Love and Steady Steps work together to give them breathing room — food support and stability coaching to help them recover and find their footing again.

  • $600 a month gives a vulnerable working family the sustained cash and grocery support to clear formal debts, build meaningful savings, and steadily journey toward self-reliance.

❤️ Migrant Workers

Migrant workers and domestic helpers build our city and our homes, often far from family and the support they know. CHoW brings them together through wellness talks, sports activities, and community outings — making Singapore a home away from home for every worker who is part of it.

  • $500 provides 185 migrant workers with welcoming safe spaces, essential wellness workshops, and the reassuring support they need to thrive while working far from home.

Leaders don’t always have to have the loudest voice in the room.

Mindy, 10, Young Hearts Programme

Once an introverted ten-year-old overwhelmed by challenges at home, Mindy found her sanctuary at Young Hearts. She has since blossomed from a quiet, distant observer into a confident mentor and leader for other children. Khai Fai has benefitted professional care and therapy programmes tailored to develop his physical, social, recreational and daily living skills.

Khai Fai, 55, Red Cross Home for the Disabled

After decades of solo caregiving, 77-year-old Mdm Wong finally found a sustainable future for her son, Khai Fai, at the Red Cross Home for the Disabled (RCHD). Living with brain damage, Khai Fai now thrives with professional therapy and a vibrant social life. While RCHD provides the high-quality care he needs, his mother finally receives the respite she deserves, knowing her son is safe and happy.

"I wouldn’t be here today without them."

Mdm Chan, 84, HoME+

Twice saved by her HoME+ panic button, Mdm Chan lives independently despite suffering from strokes and heart attacks. When sharp chest pains struck at 1 AM, one button press brought help to her doorstep in minutes.
